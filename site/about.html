What this is. What this is not.

人肉搜索 — Human Flesh Search Engine

人肉搜索 (rénròu sōusuǒ) — lit. "human flesh search engine." A form of distributed, crowdsourced investigation originating from Chinese internet culture, where thousands of anonymous participants collectively identify, locate, and document individuals or entities.

The original 人肉搜索 was raw, personal, often punitive. Mobs of netizens pursuing targets with the full collective power of distributed human attention. Sometimes righteous. Sometimes not. Always effective.

FLESHENGINE takes the architecture — distributed human intelligence collection — and redirects it. Instead of personal information, we map cognitive patterns. Instead of harassment, we produce structured analysis. The crowd sees what the algorithm misses.

The name is deliberate. The discomfort is the point. If the capability exists — and it does — it should be visible, structured, and defensible rather than hidden, chaotic, and destructive.

How This Differs from Doxing

1. Cognitive, Not Personal
We map communication patterns, narrative vulnerabilities, and network position. Not home addresses, phone numbers, or private information. The target is the public behavior, not the private person.
2. Structured Taxonomy
All observations use the FLESHENGINE influence taxonomy — 12 defined vectors with established indicators. Not free-form harassment. Every contribution maps to a framework.
3. Bidirectional
You can scan yourself. Understanding your own cognitive vulnerability surface is defensive intelligence. The tool works in both directions — analyze or be analyzed.
4. Defensive Framing
Understanding how you can be manipulated is cognitive self-defense. Every profile includes defensive recommendations. The goal is resilience, not exposure.
