Origin

人肉搜索 (rénròu sōusuǒ) — literally "human flesh search engine." Distributed, crowdsourced investigation where thousands of anonymous participants collectively identify, locate, and document targets using publicly available information and collective attention.

The practice emerged from Chinese internet culture in the early 2000s. The first documented case originated on Mop.com in 2001. By 2006 it had become a recognized social phenomenon with real consequences: people lost jobs, fled cities, were driven to suicide. The Chinese internet learned that distributed human attention, focused on a single target, is more powerful than any search algorithm.

The mechanism is simple. Someone posts a target. Thousands of people each contribute one small piece: a screenshot, a cross-referenced username, a location from a photo background, a workplace identified from a uniform. No single person does the work. The crowd does.

Western platforms developed their own versions. Doxbin. Kiwi Farms. The methods are the same. The framing is different. The capability is identical.

2006Kitten Killer of HangzhouWoman filmed crushing a kitten with high heels. Identified within days from a reflection in the video. Name, employer, phone number, address. Terminated from government position.
2008Bronze Mustache IncidentTourist photographed sitting on memorial statue. Identified within 3 hours from phone model, clothing brand, and a partial bus ticket visible in one image.
2011Vancouver Riot IDAfter the Stanley Cup riot, crowdsourced identification from social media led to 300+ people identified by name. Several faced criminal charges from crowd-submitted evidence alone.
2013Boston MarathonReddit attempted to identify the bombers from crowd photos. Wrong person identified. Sunil Tripathi, wrongly accused, was found dead. The crowd is powerful. The crowd is not always right.
2019Hong Kong 起底Both sides used doxxing extensively. Protesters identified undercover police. Pro-Beijing groups identified protesters, families, and employers. Anti-doxxing legislation passed 2021 in direct response.

What FLESHENGINE Does

FLESHENGINE structures what already happens. People already investigate each other online. The tools already exist. We provide the database, the taxonomy, and the aggregation layer.

Submit a target. The system pulls publicly available data and maps behavioral patterns, communication style, network position, and influence vulnerability across 12 vectors. Then the crowd fills in what automation misses: context, relationships, pattern shifts that only a human observer would notice.

Every fragment is timestamped, categorized, and weighted. The composite profile improves with each contribution.
